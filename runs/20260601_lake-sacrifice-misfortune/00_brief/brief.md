# 10-Minute Microfilm Brief

## Objective

Develop a 10-minute short film package from Human's new story seed.

This is a fresh, isolated Agent Team test. The task is to validate how the team process develops a new concept from Human input, not to reuse any specific creative solution from the prior run.

## Core Genre

Folk horror / dark fable / tragic ritual story.

## Core Tension

A village believes it can prevent floods by concentrating bad luck into one child and sacrificing that child to the lake god after the child turns ten. The current sacrifice is a girl who seems pitiful but may truly carry disaster around her. Her lover tries to save her. The ritual succeeds in killing her but fails to save the village.

## Creative Target

Make the story morally unstable rather than simple:

- The girl is not only an innocent victim.
- The villagers are not only cruel villains.
- The ritual may have worked for years, but its logic is monstrous.
- The ending should reveal that a system built on sacrificing one person cannot ultimately save the community.

## Constraints

- Runtime target: 10 minutes.
- Keep main characters few.
- The story must be filmable, with strong visual actions and minimal exposition.
- Avoid turning the premise into simple revenge fantasy.
- Avoid overexplaining mythology.
- The final image should reveal a new, irreversible state.
