# Concept Debate Round 1

## Inputs

- Human Creative Director seed: `00_brief/user_story_seed.md`
- Goal: not to judge the male celebrity or female celebrity, but to judge netizens, the public-opinion machine, and a suffocating downturn-era world.
- Mode: real sub-agents, not role-play.

## Screenwriter Agent Summary

The screenwriter routed to the 5-10 minute short-film workflow and proposed three directions:

### A. 未发送

An unemployed young editor takes a paid job to turn the male celebrity's "I feel uncomfortable" into a viral meme template. As he edits the material, the details of invisible bullying begin to resemble his own old wound.

Core value: the protagonist is a normal netizen-worker inside the content machine. The story judges the industrial chain of low-cost harm.

### B. 包场群

A variety-show postproduction editor realizes the same footage can be cut into two opposite narratives: "he was bullied" and "she was cyberbullied."

Core value: the story judges narrative manufacturing and the editing/public-opinion machine.

### C. 一星

A delivery rider takes a small paid task to leave a one-star review on the male celebrity's father's restaurant, then sees invisible exclusion happening in ordinary life.

Core value: the story makes online violence spill into offline bodies and small businesses.

Screenwriter recommendation: A, because one room, one computer, several clips, and several group chats can sustain a clear 10-minute dramatic action.

## Opposition Agent Summary

Major risks:

- Becoming a male-celebrity victim revenge story.
- Turning the female celebrity into a villain.
- Sliding into fan-war crisis PR.
- Becoming a real-event recap.
- Becoming a didactic anti-cyberbullying short.
- Making invisible bullying too obvious or too weak.
- Treating economic downturn as a slogan.
- Judging abstract "netizens" without giving them bodies and daily lives.

Structural defenses:

- Compress the original show background.
- Compress the female celebrity's apology.
- Remove or heavily reduce fandom combat and old controversy digging.
- Keep "I feel uncomfortable" as the trigger.
- Keep invisible bullying's deniability.
- Keep meme propagation as the main violence chain.
- Keep ordinary netizens' real-life pressure.
- Keep offline harm.
- Keep a non-celebrity mirror victim.

## Audience Agent Summary

Festival audience wants:

- Complexity, not simple villains.
- Netizens as pressured ordinary people.
- Public opinion turning from entertainment into an irresponsible machine.

Short-video audience wants:

- Immediate emotional clarity.
- Strong hook.
- Concrete harm: father's restaurant, ordinary people using the meme, silence after collapse.

AI video creator audience wants:

- Strong imageable moments.
- Not just screenshots and chat logs.
- A clear 10-minute structure.

Memorable images:

- The show has a private group without the show's star.
- "I feel uncomfortable" spreads across the city.
- The father's restaurant receives a rain of one-star reviews.
- Netizens see their own comments on a giant screen.
- The empty judge's seat: nobody is responsible, everyone participates.

## Showrunner Decision

Use **A: 未发送** as the main spine, but absorb the strongest parts of B and C.

The film should not primarily follow the male celebrity after the first act. It should follow an ordinary person inside the public-opinion economy: a recently laid-off short-video editor / former variety-show assistant who accepts a paid task to make "uncomfortable literature" go viral.

The celebrity event remains the central screen-material, but the camera's moral attention shifts to the maker and consumer of the meme.

## Working Title

《未发送》

## Current Logline

一个被裁的短视频剪辑师接到一单急活：把一位男明星的“不舒服”剪成全网玩梗模板。午夜前，他必须交片换房租；可素材里那些被排除、被对视发笑、被说“你想多了”的瞬间，逐渐和他自己的生活重叠。最后他删掉了自己的版本，却发现同样的视频已经被无数人发出。

## Core Dramatic Action

主角必须在午夜前交出一条嘲讽男明星的爆款视频，换来急需的钱。

他越剪，越发现自己不是旁观者，而是伤害链条的一环。

## Why This Best Serves The Human Goal

This direction avoids judging either celebrity as the center.

The male celebrity is not pure saint.
The female celebrity is not pure villain.
The netizen-editor is not pure evil.

The target becomes the world that turns discomfort into content, pain into traffic, and private hurt into cheap public entertainment.

## Human Decision Needed

Before moving into script structure, Human should decide:

1. Should the protagonist be a laid-off short-video editor, or a variety-show postproduction assistant?
2. Should the celebrity event stay mostly on screens, or should the opening spend 2 minutes inside the show?
3. Should the ending use the protagonist's old bullying video, the father's restaurant one-star review, or both?
