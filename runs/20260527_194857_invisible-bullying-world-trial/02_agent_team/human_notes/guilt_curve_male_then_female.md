# Human Creative Director Note: Guilt Curve

## New Direction

The film should guide audience alignment in a specific order:

1. First, make the audience feel the male celebrity is guilty.
2. Then, make the audience feel the female celebrity is guilty.
3. Finally, reveal that both judgments were shaped by editing, ordering, omission, and public-opinion framing.

## Intention

This is meant to reverse mainstream / fandom expectation.

In many public-opinion environments, audiences and fandoms may instinctively stand with the woman or assume the male celebrity is more powerful and therefore less vulnerable.

The film should use that expectation:

- First let the audience feel morally safe judging the male celebrity.
- Then shift the evidence and make the female celebrity look culpable.
- Then reveal that the viewer's certainty in both directions was manufactured.

## Showrunner Interpretation

The final target is still not the male celebrity or female celebrity.

The target is:

- the audience's hunger for a guilty person
- the ease with which gender, celebrity power, fandom, and platform framing generate preloaded judgment
- the way editing can redistribute sympathy and guilt

## Guardrail

"Male celebrity is guilty" and "female celebrity is guilty" should mean:

- The audience is led to feel guilt / culpability.
- The film temporarily frames them as guilty.

It should not mean the film ultimately declares either individual as the final moral criminal.

The ending must complicate both judgments, not flatten them.
