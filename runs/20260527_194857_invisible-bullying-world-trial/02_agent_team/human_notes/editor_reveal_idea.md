# Human Creative Director Note: Editor Reveal

## New Inspiration

Human wants the protagonist to be a postproduction editor / editor.

The film itself may initially guide the audience to stand on one side, possibly against or around the female celebrity, creating a subtle sense of audience alignment and judgment.

At the end, the audience discovers that the story they just watched was also edited by someone:

- A computer screen is visible.
- The timeline / edit project is open.
- An editor quietly leaves the workstation.

This reveal should make the audience realize again:

- They are easy to influence.
- They are easy to be guided into taking sides.
- Even the film's moral clarity may have been manufactured.

## Showrunner Interpretation

This is stronger than simply telling the audience "netizens are easily manipulated."

The structure can make the audience experience manipulation first, then recognize it.

The target of judgment becomes:

- editing as reality manufacturing
- audience appetite for certainty
- public opinion's need for villains
- the viewer's own desire to stand on the morally safe side

## Risk

The ending must not become a cheap "it was all fake" twist.

The reveal should not erase the harm. Instead, it should complicate the viewer's confidence:

The hurt may be real.
The female celebrity may have behaved badly.
The male celebrity may have been hurt.
But the audience's certainty was edited.
