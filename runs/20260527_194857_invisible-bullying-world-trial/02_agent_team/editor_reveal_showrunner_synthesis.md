# Showrunner Synthesis: Editor Reveal Feedback

## Verdict

Adopt the editor reveal structure.

But define it as an **ethical reversal**, not a **truth reversal**.

The ending must not say:

> Everything you saw was fake.

It should say:

> The pain may be real, but your certainty was edited.

## Why This Is Strong

The user's goal is not to judge the male celebrity or female celebrity.

The new structure lets the film judge:

- public-opinion manufacturing
- editing as reality arrangement
- viewers' desire to stand on a safe moral side
- platforms and clients demanding clarity, anger, and villains
- the downturn-era pressure that makes people sell certainty for rent

It also turns the audience into part of the experiment. They do not only watch people being led by public opinion; they experience being led.

## Adopted Protagonist

Use a hybrid:

**A former variety-show postproduction editor, now unemployed / outsourced, taking short-video public-opinion jobs.**

Why:

- Variety-show background gives him access to multi-camera footage and knowledge of how relationships are manufactured.
- Short-video outsourcing gives him economic pressure and a reason to produce aggressive, viral edits.
- This identity links the two machines: variety shows manufacture intimacy; short videos manufacture judgment.

## Updated Dramatic Action

The protagonist must cut a public-opinion video before midnight.

The task is not simply to smear or defend someone.

The task is to manufacture a version clear enough for viewers to:

- stand on one side
- feel morally clean
- repost
- punish someone

His obstacle is not lack of material. His obstacle is that every edit creates a plausible criminal.

## Required Guardrails

1. Do not erase the male celebrity's hurt.
2. Do not make the female celebrity a pure villain.
3. Do not make the editor a mastermind villain.
4. Do not reveal that everything was fake.
5. Seed editing language from the beginning.
6. Let the audience stand somewhere, then make them doubt the cleanliness of that stance.
7. End on the machine continuing, not on a speech.

## Recommended Ending

Use a blend of Audience versions one and three:

The film returns to a fictional editing interface.

The timeline contains the film we just watched, named something like:

- `justice_version_final_03`
- `female_reaction_suspicious`
- `male_silence_extended`
- `explanation_cut_too_long`
- `group_laughter_peak_v7`

Nearby are alternate timelines:

- `male_self_pity_version`
- `female_counterattack_version`
- `netizen_trial_version`
- `warm_reconciliation_version`

The editor selects the main sequence, hesitates, and deletes it.

For one second the timeline is empty.

Then a message arrives:

> Project copied.  
> New version published.  
> Heat rising.

He leaves. The chair is empty. The screen remains on.
